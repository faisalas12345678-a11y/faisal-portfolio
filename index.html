UX Case Study · Graduation Project

ToYou Food Delivery — UX Redesign

In Progress Role: UX Researcher / Designer Figma Surveys · 60 responses Usability Testing · 5 sessions

Overview

This Human–Computer Interaction graduation project investigates the user experience of the ToYou food delivery app, focusing specifically on exploratory users — people who browse without a specific order in mind and rely on the app to help them decide.

Problem Statement

  • The current interface is store-centric, serving users who already know what they want.
  • The homepage is visually overloaded, increasing cognitive load from the first screen.
  • Layered decision structures and unclear menus push users to leave the app and search externally before ordering.
  • Customization flows lack clarity, creating confusion at a critical point in the journey.

Research & Methods

  • Heuristic evaluation and cognitive walkthrough of the live app
  • Task-based UX analysis and information architecture review
  • Survey data from 60 respondents capturing real usage patterns and frustrations
  • 5 usability testing sessions with real participants
  • UX journey map of the current experience
  • Benchmark analysis of HungerStation, Jahez, and Mrsool
  • Personas and storyboard development based on research findings

Key Design Decisions

Low-fidelity wireframes · Homepage → Search → Item → Restaurant → Cart → Payment
  • Restructuring the homepage to surface discovery-first content rather than a promotional store list
  • Simplifying the information architecture to reduce decision layers
  • Introducing a more guided browsing path for users who haven't decided what to order yet
  • Improving menu clarity to lower abandonment at the customization stage

Current outcomes: Clearer browsing structure, reduced cognitive load in the main flow, and a stronger IA foundation for the redesigned experience. The project is actively in progress.

Reflection

This project reinforced that interface problems are rarely surface-level. The visual overload on ToYou's homepage was a symptom of a deeper IA problem — one that research made visible and systematic redesign can address.

Banking UX Redesign

Bank Al Bilad — Beneficiary Addition Flow

Completed Nov 29 – Dec 18, 2025 Role: UX Designer Figma HCI Principles Flow Optimization

Overview

This case study focuses on a single, high-friction flow in the Bank Al Bilad mobile app: adding a new beneficiary for a bank transfer. The original flow placed unnecessary steps in front of users during a sensitive task, creating avoidable friction and reducing confidence.

Problem Statement

  • The original flow spanned 7 screens to complete a single transactional task.
  • Fields like "relationship" and "physical address" added length without adding value to the transfer process.
  • Unnecessary decision points increased perceived complexity in an already high-stakes interaction.
  • The flow created friction at a moment when users expect speed and reliability.

Design Process

  • Mapped the original 7-screen flow step by step
  • Identified fields and screens that did not contribute to completing the task
  • Applied HCI principles around minimizing steps in task-critical flows
  • Redesigned the flow to 5 screens by removing non-essential inputs
  • Validated the revised flow against the original for completeness and efficiency

Before → After

Before: 7 screens  →  After: 5 screens  ·  −28% reduction in flow length
Open Figma Prototype ↗

Outcome: The redesigned flow reduced screen count from 7 to 5 (−28%), removed non-essential fields (relationship, physical address), and produced a faster, cleaner beneficiary-addition experience grounded in HCI principles.

Reflection

This project demonstrated how much clarity can be gained simply by removing what doesn't belong. In transactional UX, every extra step is a trust cost. The discipline was in identifying what was truly necessary versus what had accumulated by habit.

Data Visualization · Power BI

Multi-Persona Dashboard System

Completed Role: UX / Information Designer Power BI Information Design Data Visualization

Overview

This project addresses a common data visualization failure: building one dashboard and expecting everyone to use it. Instead, this system was designed around three distinct user personas — each with a different relationship to data, a different decision context, and a different need for depth.

The Three Personas

  • CEO (Strategic View): KPI summary, long-term trend lines, minimal interaction. The dashboard answers: "Are we on track?"
  • Operational Manager: Monthly performance, target comparisons, recent trend alerts, and areas needing immediate attention. Answers: "What needs action now?"
  • Senior Data Analyst: Deeper diagnostic views, segment comparisons, filtering controls, and analytical reasoning support. Answers: "Why is this happening?"

The Three Dashboards

CEO View · KPI summary, YoY growth, profit by country, sales trend · Minimal interaction
Manager View · MTD performance, sales vs target, 6-month trend, segment filter
Analyst View · Profit margin by segment, segment comparison, country & discount-band filters
Open Interactive Power BI Dashboard ↗
  • Information hierarchy matched to each persona's decision speed and depth requirement
  • The CEO view intentionally removes filtering and granular controls — simplicity is the feature
  • The Analyst view surfaces comparison controls and segment breakdowns unavailable in the other views
  • Color and layout consistent across views for brand coherence; depth and density varied by persona

Outcome: Three dashboards, one coherent system. Strategic, operational, and analytical needs addressed without compromise — each user sees exactly what serves their decisions.

Reflection

The most valuable design decision in this project was choosing what to hide. A dashboard that shows everything serves no one well. Designing for a persona means designing a point of view — and defending it.
